I am a Ph.D. student in Statistics at Tel Aviv University, supervised by Prof. Daniel Nevo. My research focuses on causal inference, with a particular emphasis on the intersection of causal inference and survival analysis. Specifically, my current research explores partial identification of vaccine efficacy effects and analyzing real longitudinal medical data with time-dependent confounders. My doctoral studies are generously supported by the Milner Foundation for excellent doctoral students in STEM.

Before starting my Ph.D., I worked as a statistician in various fields. At the Israel Center for Disease Control (ICDC), I analyzed dietary patterns in the Israeli population, and at the Gastroenterology Department for Children in SMZC Hospital, I focused on data analysis for the IBD registry.
